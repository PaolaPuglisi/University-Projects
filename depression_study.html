PART 1: SELECTING DATA¶

For this assignment we decided to work with Depression data. As first we downloaded the ground truth data from the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System, for the years from 2013 to 2016. Then we get the Google Trend data, selecting some keywords, related to the Depression desease. The keyword selected are: 'Panic','Zoloft','Lexapro','SSRI','Prozac','Cymbalta','Anxiety','Dopamine','Antidepressant','Self esteem','Happiness','Xanax','Pain', 'Irritability','Sad','Psychotherapy and 'Fatigue'.

We downloaded the data from Google using the Pytrends package, because it seemed to be more convenient and more efficient. However, during the performance of the task, we realized that Google Trends modifies the data (even those of years old) and that therefore, the results did not coincide with the previous days. So it's possible to have differences on results, but for day it changes just of few percent points for the predictions.Hence, the errors on the models can change, but the general results and the general considerations carried out definitely remain valid.

We built the following four datasets (df13, df14, df15, df16) having the Google Trend data for each year and for each state of the US. As first we fill the missing values with the mean of each state.

PART 2: PREDICTION MODEL¶

For the prediction we use two kind of crossvalidation: Temporal, to predict the morbidity rate on a given year and Spatial, to predict the morbidity rate on a given State.

Temporal Predictions¶

We started with the prediction on the Age prevalence target. As first we normalized data using standard normalization. Then we chose to compute the prediction on 2014, 2015, 2016. To do this we built the datasets in this way: for each of the three prediction, we concatenated the rows of the dataframe having all the keywords and the real data. So that, we predict the given year on the basis of the previous ones. As result, we get three dataframes called "TrendTot"(for the prediction on 2016),"TrendTotN13" (for the prediction on 2014),"TrendTotN14" (for the prediction on 2015), we decided to use these as training set. The test set are called "Target", "TargetN13", "TargetN14", regarding the year chosen for the prediction.

It means that, if model doesn't know totally the behaviour of disease in a State, neither in other years, predictions on that states are not reliable.

Prediction with Crude Prevalence¶

Now we can see the same procedure for the Crude prevalence, we begin with Temporal Crossvalidation for the year 2016. The crude prevalence is not a weighted average as the age adjusted prevalence, so it's less accurated then the previous one. In fact Age adjusted prevalence rates is a way to make fairer comparisons between groups with different age distributions. For example, a county having a higher percentage of elderly people may have a higher rate of death or hospitalization than a county with a younger population, merely because the old people are more likely to die or be hospitalized. In the light of this, we have that results show that the RMSE for the Crude prevalence is higher, and the Rsquare is lower. So we can conclude that using an age prevalnce gives more accurate predictions.
